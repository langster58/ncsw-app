# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Car owners in Northeast Ohio deciding whether to trust a shop with a serious audio install, from ground-pound bass heads to SQ listeners. They browse the way they browse Edmunds or MotorTrend: pick their exact car, expect specifics. They are customers, not installers — the site is never a DIY resource or an install reference.

## Product Purpose

Sell pre-engineered, pre-priced sound-system packages built for the customer's exact vehicle. The page's job is to make one impression land: this shop already knows your car — its cabin volumes, its factory signal path, its measured cargo bay, its charging system — before you ever call. Success is a scheduled install, driven by the sheer thoroughness of the accounting.

## Brand Personality

Measured, engineering-confident, ledger-honest. "The car goes on record." Loud is legitimate business, stated without apology. Prices are itemized and explained before they're asked about.

## Anti-references

- Crutchfield-style fit-guide/resource pages: we display facts as proof of mastery, not as a lookup tool.
- DIY forum how-to framing. Nothing on the page teaches the install.
- SPL-shop neon/flame aesthetics and retail urgency banners.
- Generic AI landing-page scaffolding.

## Design Principles

- **A fact only appears attached to a decision.** Bare specs read as a resource; a measurement inside a judgment reads as professionalism.
- **Copy is written at the grain where the fact lives.** Vehicle-grain lines are generated from data; platform-grain paragraphs (factory_integration) are written once per audio platform; component copy is written once per component; packages assemble, never get bespoke prose.
- **Familiar formats, repurposed.** The automotive spec-strip idiom every car shopper knows, filled with package-specific facts.
- **Evidence only.** Never display a number the database doesn't hold. Honest empty states over invented values.
- **One ledger.** Components, labor, and upgrades itemized in one currency; the arithmetic shown before it's questioned.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA contrast on all text; reduced-motion honored for any entrance animation; data conveyed in text, never color alone.
